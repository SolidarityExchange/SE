How to Send a Letter

If your first letter gets through, odds are you’ll write again—and that's the goal. Sending a letter is one of the simplest, most sincere forms of support. This guide walks you through the rules and logistics so your mail makes it to its destination.


Not sure what to say? Click here for suggestions.


1. Pick Someone to Write

Start with our Prisoner Registry. We include current mailing addresses and any facility-specific notes when available.


2. Follow the Rules

Most facilities require:

  • White,single-sided paper only using one First-Class Mail Forever stamp
  • No more than 5 pages. (5 pages of 20 lb paper in a plain envelope typically weighs 1 oz or less.
  • Typed or handwritten is fine, but use black or blue ink only
  • No glitter, glue, stickers, or decorative items
  • Cards, Polaroids, and printed photos may be allowed, but double check first.
  • No perfume, stains, or smudges.


Simply put, print or hand write on regular 8.5 in x 11 in paper and use a plain #10 envelope. Follow these rules and you will be successful in writing your letter.


3. Include Your Return Address

Your return address must appear on the envelope and it recommended that you write it on the letter itself. The reason for this is that some prisoners have their mail scanned, and you will want them to have your address for reference.

If you prefer not to use your home address, consider a PO box or our Email Proxy.


4. Send It

Use standard postage. Assume staff may read it—so keep things respectful and clear. That said, it doesn’t need to be formal. Simple support matters.
