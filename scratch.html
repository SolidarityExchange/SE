Supporting Prisoners

You can support political prisoners by sending letters through the mail, using our email-to-letter proxy service, or contributing commissary funds. These small acts of solidarity can make a real difference in the lives of incarcerated people.


Before you write or donate, you’ll often need to confirm where someone is being held. We work hard to provide accurate information in our prisoner registry, but mistakes happen—if you notice an error or outdated info, please double-check with the official sources provided below and let us know so we can fix it.

Finding Federal Prisoners

You can search for individuals in federal prisons using the Federal Inmate Locator.

Finding State Prisoners

Each state has its own inmate lookup system. Click here for a list of state prisoner lookup tools (coming soon).

Finding County Jail Detainees

For county jails, you may need to check the jail’s official website. Search online for the county name and "inmate lookup" or "jail roster." Many county jails have online search tools.

Using VINELink as a 'catch-all'

VINELink is a national service that allows you to locate most state and local prisoners as well as some federal prisoners. You will need to enter which state you suspect the prisoner is in.

You can also receive notifications about their custody status. You can access it at VINELink.com.
